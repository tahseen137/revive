Stripe Smart Retries vs Custom Dunning: Which Recovers More Revenue?

Stripe Smart Retries are free. They're already enabled on your account. They use machine learning to automatically retry failed subscription payments at the optimal time.

So why would you pay for custom dunning software?

Because Smart Retries only recover 20-30% of failed payments. Custom dunning recovers 60-70%.

That's the difference between losing $7,000/month and losing $3,000/month on a $100K MRR business.

Here's why — and when you should make the switch.

What Stripe Smart Retries Actually Do

Smart Retries is Stripe's built-in payment recovery system. When a subscription payment fails, Stripe automatically:

  • Schedules retry attempts over the next 2-3 weeks
  • Uses machine learning trained on billions of transactions to pick the best retry timing
  • Adjusts retry logic based on decline code (insufficient funds get different timing than expired cards)
  • Stops retrying after a certain number of failures to avoid annoying customers

All of this happens invisibly in the background. No setup required.

What Smart Retries DON'T Do

Here's the problem: Smart Retries only handle the retry logic. They don't:

  • ❌ Send emails to customers explaining the failure
  • ❌ Provide a one-click link to update payment info
  • ❌ Give customers a heads-up before cancellation
  • ❌ Let you customize retry timing for your business
  • ❌ Track what's working (you can't A/B test or optimize)

Think about it from the customer's perspective:

Their card fails on March 1st. Stripe retries on March 3rd. It fails again. Stripe retries on March 7th. Succeeds.

The customer never got an email. They don't know their card failed. They don't know Stripe retried. They just see two charges on their statement and wonder if they got double-billed.

Or worse: the retries all fail, Stripe gives up after 2 weeks, and the subscription gets canceled. The customer wanted to stay — they just forgot to update their expired card. But they never got a reminder.

What Custom Dunning Adds

Custom dunning software (like Revive, ChurnKey, or ProfitWell Retain) does everything Smart Retries do, PLUS:

1. Immediate Customer Notification

Within 1 hour of payment failure, the customer gets an email:

Subject: Quick: Payment method needs updating

Hi Sarah,

We tried to charge your card ending in 4242 for your $49 Pro plan, but the payment didn't go through.

Usually this happens when a card expires or your bank flags the transaction. No worries — you can fix it in 30 seconds:

[Update Payment Method]

Your account is still active for the next 7 days while we sort this out.

Thanks,
Team

This email alone recovers 40-50% of failed payments. Customers WANT to stay — they just didn't know there was a problem.

2. One-Click Payment Update

Smart Retries assume the customer will eventually log in and update their card. They won't.

Custom dunning sends a direct link to Stripe's Billing Portal or a checkout page. One click, update the card, done. No login required.

This reduces friction by 90%. Update rates go from 15% (with login) to 60% (one-click).

3. Multi-Email Dunning Sequence

If the first email doesn't work, send 2 more:

  • Email 1 (Day 1): Friendly heads-up, easy fix
  • Email 2 (Day 5): Urgency, account at risk
  • Email 3 (Day 10): Final notice, cancellation in 48 hours

Recovery by email:

  • Email 1: 45% of recoveries
  • Email 2: 30% of recoveries
  • Email 3: 25% of recoveries

Without the sequence, you lose 55% of recoveries that would have happened later.

4. Decline Code-Specific Routing

Smart Retries treat all failures the same. Custom dunning routes by decline code:

Decline Code Smart Retries Custom Dunning
card_expired Retry in 3 days (will fail again) No retry, immediate email with update link
insufficient_funds Retry next day (still no money) Wait 3-5 days (next paycheck), then retry
do_not_honor Retry in 3 days Email asking customer to contact bank or use different card

By routing correctly, you recover 15-20% more than blind retries.

5. Grace Period + In-App Prompts

Smart Retries cancel the subscription after 2-3 weeks of failures. Custom dunning gives customers a grace period:

  • Keep access for 7-14 days (read-only mode)
  • Show an in-app banner prompting payment update
  • Send email reminders every 3 days

This recovers another 10-15% who would otherwise churn.

When Smart Retries Are Good Enough

Stripe Smart Retries are fine if:

  • You're pre-revenue or under $1K MRR (the ROI of dunning software isn't there yet)
  • Your failed payment rate is under 3% (rare, but some businesses have this)
  • You're testing pricing and don't want to add complexity yet

But once you hit $5K MRR, you should upgrade. Here's why:

The Math at $5K MRR

  • Failed payment rate: 9% (industry average) = $450/month lost
  • Smart Retries (25% recovery): $113 recovered
  • Still losing: $337/month = $4,044/year

Add custom dunning:

  • Recovery rate: 65%
  • Recovered: $293/month
  • Cost: $49/month (Revive)
  • Net gain: $244/month = $2,928/year

That's a 60x ROI in year one.

When to Upgrade to Custom Dunning

Upgrade if:

  • ✅ You're making $5K+ MRR
  • ✅ Your failed payment rate is above 5%

Don't upgrade if:

  • ❌ You're pre-revenue
  • ❌ You're still validating your product idea

How to Set Up Custom Dunning

If you're switching from Smart Retries to custom dunning, here's the process:

Option 1: Use a Tool (10 minutes)

  1. Sign up for Revive, ChurnKey, or ProfitWell Retain
  2. Connect your Stripe account (OAuth, takes 30 seconds)
  3. Customize email templates (or use defaults)
  4. Turn on dunning
  5. Monitor recovery rate in the dashboard

Option 2: Build Your Own (2-4 hours)

If you want full control, build your own dunning system:

  1. Set up Stripe webhooks — listen for invoice.payment_failed
  2. Send immediate email — use SendGrid or Postmark with a one-click update link
  3. Schedule retries — use a cron job to check for past_due invoices and retry based on decline code
  4. Build dunning sequence — 3 emails over 7-14 days using a delayed job queue (Sidekiq, BullMQ, etc.)
  5. Track results — log recovery rate by decline code, email performance, revenue saved

Building your own gives you 100% control, but costs 10-20 hours to build + maintain. Most teams are better off using a tool.

Common Mistakes When Switching

1. Keeping Stripe Smart Retries enabled alongside custom dunning
You'll end up retrying twice on the same day. Turn off Smart Retries once you enable custom dunning (or configure your tool to coordinate with Stripe's retries).

2. Using the same email for all decline codes
Expired cards need a different message than insufficient funds. Route by decline code.

3. Retrying too aggressively
Don't retry insufficient funds cases every day. Wait 3-5 days between attempts.

4. Making customers log in to update their card
Use Stripe Billing Portal or a direct SetupIntent link. One-click updates recover 4x more than login-required flows.

5. Not A/B testing email copy
Small changes in subject lines and CTAs can boost recovery by 10-20%. Test constantly.

What to Track

Once you switch to custom dunning, track these metrics:

  • Overall recovery rate: % of failed payments recovered (target: 60-70%)
  • Recovery rate by decline code: expired cards should hit 80%+, insufficient funds 50-60%
  • Email performance: Open rate (target: 55-65%), click rate (target: 20-30%)
  • Time to recovery: How long it takes to recover (faster = better)
  • Revenue recovered per month: The dollar amount saved (this is your ROI metric)

If your recovery rate is under 50%, something's broken. Check:

  • Are emails going to spam?
  • Is the payment update link working?
  • Are you giving customers enough time (7-14 days before cancellation)?
