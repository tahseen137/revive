# SaaS Churn Benchmarks 2026: What's a Good Churn Rate for Your Stage?

*12 min read · Churn & Retention*

"What's a good churn rate?" is the question every SaaS founder asks at 2am while staring at their analytics dashboard.

The honest answer: it depends on your stage, pricing, and whether you're measuring voluntary or involuntary churn. A 5% monthly churn rate might be catastrophic for an enterprise SaaS and totally normal for a $9/month consumer app.

Here's the definitive breakdown of SaaS churn benchmarks for 2026, segmented by company stage — plus what separates the top performers from the rest.

---

## The Headline Numbers: 2026 SaaS Churn Benchmarks

| Company Stage | Monthly Churn (Median) | Monthly Churn (Top Quartile) | Annual Churn (Median) |
|--------------|------------------------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| Pre-PMF (<$1M ARR) | 6-8% | <4% | 50-65% |
| Early Growth ($1-10M ARR) | 3-5% | <2.5% | 30-45% |
| Scale ($10-50M ARR) | 2-3% | <1.5% | 20-30% |
| Enterprise ($50M+ ARR) | 0.5-1.5% | <0.8% | 5-15% |

**Critical caveat:** These are blended rates. Your benchmark depends heavily on your business model, ACV, and customer segment.

---

## Pre-Product-Market Fit (Under $1M ARR)

At this stage, **churn is learning disguised as a metric.**

If you're under $1M ARR, your churn rate is mostly noise. You have too few customers for statistical significance, and you're still figuring out who your ideal customer is. High churn often means you're selling to the wrong people — which is valuable data.

**What's normal:**
- Monthly churn: 6-8%
- Expect significant variance month-to-month
- Some cohorts will churn at 20%, others at 2%

**What to actually focus on:**
- **Cohort analysis over aggregate rates.** Which customer types stick?
- **Cancellation reasons.** Are you losing customers to competitors, budget cuts, or product gaps?
- **Time to first value.** Users who get value in week 1 rarely churn.

**Red flag:** If your best-fit customers (the ones you think should love you) are churning at 5%+/month, you don't have product-market fit yet.

---

## Early Growth Stage ($1-10M ARR)

This is where churn starts mattering. At $5M ARR with 5% monthly churn, you're losing $250K/month. That's real money.

**2026 benchmarks:**
- Monthly churn: 3-5% (median)
- Top performers: <2.5%
- Logo churn matters more than revenue churn at this stage

**What separates good from great:**

The difference between 5% and 2.5% monthly churn is the difference between a company that struggles to grow and one that compounds. At 5% monthly churn, you need to add 60% of your base each year just to stay flat. At 2.5%, that drops to 30%.

**Focus areas:**
- **Onboarding optimization.** Most churn happens in the first 30-90 days.
- **Customer success for your top 20%.** High-touch doesn't scale, so prioritize.
- **Payment failure recovery.** At this stage, 20-40% of churn is involuntary (failed payments). This is low-hanging fruit.

---

## Scale Stage ($10-50M ARR)

At scale, churn management becomes a dedicated function. You have enough data to run real experiments and enough customers that 1% churn improvement equals millions in ARR.

**2026 benchmarks:**
- Monthly churn: 2-3% (median)
- Top performers: <1.5%
- Net revenue retention becomes the key metric (>100% means expansion exceeds churn)

**The math that matters:**

At $20M ARR with 2.5% monthly churn, you're losing $6M/year. Cutting that to 1.5% saves $2.4M annually. That's a full engineering team worth of salary — just from reducing churn by 1 point.

**What top performers do differently:**
- Dedicated retention teams
- Predictive churn models
- Proactive outreach before customers consider canceling
- Sophisticated dunning systems for payment recovery

---

## Enterprise Stage ($50M+ ARR)

Enterprise SaaS plays by different rules. Long contracts, higher switching costs, and dedicated account management fundamentally change the churn equation.

**2026 benchmarks:**
- Monthly churn: 0.5-1.5%
- Annual churn: 5-15%
- Net revenue retention: 110-130%+ for top performers

At this stage, **net revenue retention (NRR)** matters more than gross churn. Atlassian's 130%+ NRR means they can lose some customers and still grow revenue from existing accounts through expansion.

**Enterprise dynamics:**
- Logo churn often driven by M&A, bankruptcy, or strategic changes — not product issues
- Revenue churn can be lower than logo churn (smaller customers leave, larger ones stay)
- Contraction (downgrades) can be as important as outright cancellation

---

## Voluntary vs. Involuntary Churn: The Hidden Split

Here's the insight most benchmarks miss: **not all churn is created equal.**

**Voluntary churn:** Customer consciously decides to cancel
- Driven by: poor product fit, budget cuts, competitor switch, product gaps
- Fix with: better positioning, feature development, customer success

**Involuntary churn:** Payment fails and customer never returns
- Driven by: expired cards, insufficient funds, bank errors, fraud flags
- Fix with: dunning optimization, payment retry logic, card updaters

**The breakdown by stage:**

| Stage | Voluntary | Involuntary |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Pre-PMF | 70% | 30% |
| Early Growth | 65% | 35% |
| Scale | 60% | 40% |
| Enterprise | 85% | 15% |

Why does involuntary churn increase at scale? More customers means more payment diversity, more card expirations, and more edge cases. Enterprise is lower because they typically pay by invoice/ACH.

**The opportunity:** Involuntary churn is largely fixable with good infrastructure. Companies using optimized dunning systems recover 50-70% of failed payments. That's pure found money.

## How to Calculate Your Churn Rate (The Right Way)

There are multiple ways to calculate churn, and they can give wildly different numbers.

**Logo churn (customer churn):**
```
Logo Churn % = (Customers Lost / Starting Customers) × 100
```

**Revenue churn (MRR churn):**
```
Revenue Churn % = (MRR Lost / Starting MRR) × 100
```

**Net revenue churn (accounts for expansion):**
```
Net Revenue Churn % = (MRR Lost - Expansion MRR) / Starting MRR × 100
```

**Which one to use?**
- Pre-PMF: Logo churn (too few customers for revenue churn to be meaningful)
- Early Growth: Both logo and revenue
- Scale+: Net revenue retention (the inverse of net revenue churn)

## What Impacts Churn More Than Stage

Your stage matters, but these factors matter more:

**1. Contract length**
- Monthly contracts: 4-6% monthly churn typical
- Annual contracts: 0.5-1% monthly churn typical
- The same company can have 4x different churn rates based on contract type

**2. Price point**
- <$50/month: Higher churn (lower commitment, easier to cancel)
- $500+/month: Lower churn (more integration, higher switching cost)

**3. Buyer vs. User**
- User = buyer: Higher churn (individual budget decisions)
- User ≠ buyer: Lower churn (procurement processes create stickiness)

**4. Integration depth**
- Standalone tools: Higher churn
- Tools integrated into workflow: Lower churn
- Platforms others build on: Lowest churn

## Takeaways: Where Should You Actually Be?

1. **Compare to your segment, not all SaaS.** A $10/month B2C product and a $10K/month enterprise product have nothing in common except being SaaS.

2. **Track voluntary vs. involuntary separately.** Mixing them hides the real story.

3. **Aim for top quartile, not median.** Median SaaS companies struggle. Top quartile companies compound.

4. **Fix involuntary churn first.** It's cheaper and faster than fixing product-market fit. Good dunning systems can cut your churn by 20-30% without changing your product.

5. **Use cohorts, not aggregates.** Your January cohort and December cohort might behave completely differently.
